# Quiz: On Impermanence and Letting Go

**Q1 · On changing states** — *You experience a profound moment of peace during practice, but it fades the next day. What is the most skillful response?*
- · Try desperately to recreate the exact conditions of that sit.
- · Feel like a failure because you couldn't hold onto the peace.
- ✓ Notice the passing of the peace, recognizing that all states—even beautiful ones—arise and fall.
- · Stop meditating until you feel guaranteed to experience that peace again.
- *Gentle*: The practice is not about building a permanent house of peace, but learning to sit comfortably in the weather, knowing every storm and every sunny day will pass.

**Q2 · On letting go** — *What does "letting go" actually mean in the context of contemplative practice?*
- · Apathetically giving up on your goals and desires.
- · Forcing yourself to stop caring about the people you love.
- ✓ Releasing the tight mental grip on how things "should" be, and allowing them to be as they are.
- · Denying that you feel any pain or attachment.
- *Gentle*: Letting go is not pushing away. It is simply opening the hand. It is the relaxing of the demand that this moment be anything other than what it is.

**Q3 · On grief and loss** — *How does an understanding of impermanence support us in times of grief?*
- · It makes the grief disappear instantly because we know it was inevitable.
- · It tells us that our feelings don't matter because nothing lasts anyway.
- ✓ It provides a spacious container for the pain, reminding us that even this heavy sorrow is part of the moving river of life.
- · It allows us to bypass the sadness and jump straight to acceptance.
- *Gentle*: Impermanence does not make loss hurt less, but it removes the shock that things have changed. It allows us to mourn with grace, knowing that change is the very nature of existence.

**Q4 · On clinging to joy** — *Why can holding tightly to a positive experience cause suffering?*
- · Because positive experiences are inherently bad for us.
- · Because it makes other people jealous.
- ✓ Because fighting to keep something that must eventually change creates immediate tension and fear of loss.
- · Because you don't deserve to be happy for too long.
- *Gentle*: When we try to freeze a beautiful moment, we crush it. Joy is a bird that lands on the windowsill; we can enjoy its song, but we suffer if we try to trap it in a cage.

**Q5 · On the breath as a teacher** — *How does the simple act of breathing teach us about impermanence?*
- · By showing us how long we can hold our breath.
- ✓ By showing that we must exhale what we have just inhaled to survive; we cannot hold onto a single breath forever.
- · By proving that our lung capacity decreases over time.
- · By distracting us from the passage of time.
- *Gentle*: The breath is the rhythm of life itself: taking in, letting go, taking in, letting go. Holding your breath brings panic; exhaling brings release.

**Q6 · On aging and the body** — *How do we skillfully relate to the changing, aging nature of our physical bodies?*
- · By obsessively trying to look and feel young forever.
- · By ignoring the body completely and living only in the mind.
- · By feeling resentful and betrayed by the body's decline.
- ✓ By turning toward the changes with tenderness, honoring the body for its service rather than demanding it remain static.
- *Gentle*: The body is a landscape that changes over time. We can either fight the changing seasons, or we can learn to appreciate the unique beauty and quiet wisdom of autumn and winter.
