# Quiz: On Grounding and Presence

**Q1 · On physical sensation** — *When feeling ungrounded or swept away by thought, where is it often most helpful to direct your attention?*
- ✓ To the physical sensation of the feet resting on the floor or the body in the chair.
- · To analyzing the thoughts that are making you feel ungrounded.
- · To planning out the rest of your day to regain control.
- · To remembering a time when you felt perfectly calm.
- *Gentle*: The body is always in the present moment. Noticing physical contact is a direct anchor back to the "now" when the mind has traveled elsewhere.

**Q2 · On the present moment** — *What does it mean to "be present"?*
- · To completely empty the mind of all thoughts and feelings.
- · To force yourself to only think positive, uplifting thoughts.
- ✓ To gently acknowledge and experience whatever is happening right now, without needing to change it.
- · To ignore the past and the future entirely in your daily life.
- *Gentle*: Presence is not an empty mind, but a welcoming awareness. It is the simple, quiet knowing of what is here.

**Q3 · On distraction** — *You realize you've been lost in a daydream for several minutes during your sit. What is the most grounding action?*
- · Scold yourself for losing focus to ensure it doesn't happen again.
- · Try to forcefully push the daydream out of your mind.
- ✓ Simply note that thinking has occurred, and warmly return to your anchor.
- · Follow the daydream to its logical conclusion before returning.
- *Gentle*: Waking up from a thought is the moment of practice. The return itself is the muscle we are building, and it requires only warmth, not tension.

**Q4 · On bodily tension** — *You notice a tight, uncomfortable sensation in your shoulders while sitting. How do you respond?*
- · Immediately try to stretch it out and fix the posture.
- · Tell yourself the pain isn't real and try to ignore it.
- · Focus all your willpower on forcing the muscles to relax.
- ✓ Soften your awareness around the tension, allowing it to be there without adding resistance.
- *Gentle*: Often, our resistance to discomfort creates more tension than the discomfort itself. Meeting it with a soft awareness can be profoundly releasing.

**Q5 · On environmental noise** — *A loud, unexpected sound interrupts your quiet space. What is the practice here?*
- · Become frustrated that your peaceful environment was ruined.
- ✓ Let the sound become the new object of your meditation for a moment, noticing its arising and passing.
- · Block your ears or try to find a completely silent room.
- · Stop the meditation, as the conditions are no longer perfect.
- *Gentle*: The world will always have sound. When we stop demanding silence, every sound becomes a bell ringing us back to the present.

**Q6 · On the feeling of weight** — *Why is noticing gravity often used as a grounding tool?*
- · Because it helps you fall asleep faster.
- · Because it requires intense intellectual focus to understand.
- ✓ Because the downward pull is a constant, steady, and reliable physical reality you can always tune into.
- · Because it distracts you from emotional pain by focusing on physical pain.
- *Gentle*: Gravity asks nothing of us. Noticing the chair holding you up is a gentle reminder that, right now, you are supported and you do not need to hold everything together.
